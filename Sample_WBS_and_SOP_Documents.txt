SOP DOCUMENT 1: Hot Metal Sampling and Temperature Measurement
---

SOP Number: BF-QC-003
Title: Blast Furnace Hot Metal Sampling Procedure
Revision: 4.2
Effective Date: January 2026

SAFETY REQUIREMENTS:
All personnel involved in hot metal sampling must wear complete PPE including aluminised suit, face shield, safety helmet, safety shoes, and heat-resistant gloves at all times. This is safety critical and non-negotiable.

PROCEDURE STEPS:

Step 1: Pre-sampling preparation by Sample Operator
Sample operator to verify that sampling equipment is ready - sample spoon, sample moulds, temperature probe, and PPE are in good condition. Equipment check must be completed before furnace tap begins.

Step 2: Communication and coordination by Shift Supervisor
Shift supervisor to coordinate with furnace tapper and confirm sampling point and timing. Notify quality laboratory that sample will be ready for collection in approximately 15 minutes.

Step 3: First sample collection at 5-minute mark by Sample Operator
Wait until hot metal flow is steady (approximately 5 minutes after tap starts). Using long-handled sampling spoon, collect hot metal sample from runner stream. Pour immediately into prepared cast iron sample moulds (3 samples minimum). SAFETY CRITICAL: Maintain safe distance, avoid any water contact with hot metal.

Step 4: Temperature measurement by Sample Operator
Immediately after taking sample, immerse calibrated immersion-type temperature probe into hot metal stream. Hold probe in stream for 10 seconds minimum for accurate reading. Record temperature on sampling log sheet.

Step 5: Sample identification and logging by Sample Operator
Mark each sample mould with furnace number, tap number, date, time, and temperature whilst metal is still molten (markings will be permanent). Complete sampling log with all required information including operator name and supervisor signature.

Step 6: Second sample collection at 20-minute mark by Sample Operator
Repeat Steps 3-5 to collect mid-tap sample. This provides check for consistency across the tap.

Step 7: Sample cooling and transfer by Sample Operator
Allow samples to cool in moulds for minimum 30 minutes. Once cooled and safe to handle, transfer samples to laboratory sample trolley. Notify laboratory that samples are ready for analysis.

Step 8: Laboratory notification and documentation by Shift Supervisor
Shift supervisor to inform quality laboratory supervisor of sample availability. Ensure all documentation is complete and accompanies samples.

SOP DOCUMENT 2: Continuous Casting Machine Startup Sequence
---

SOP Number: CCM-OP-011
Title: CCM Cold Startup After Shutdown
Department: Steel Making Shop - Continuous Casting
Approved by: Chief Operations Officer

PREREQUISITES:
This procedure applies only after planned shutdown of 8 hours or more when CCM has cooled down. For normal operation and sequence changes, refer to SOP-CCM-OP-012.

Step 1: Pre-startup inspection by Mechanical Fitter
Mechanical fitter to inspect all mechanical components - mould oscillation system, segment alignment, spray cooling nozzles, and withdrawal rolls. Any defects must be reported to shift in-charge before proceeding. Safety guards must be in position and secured.

Step 2: Tundish preheating initiation by Tundish Operator
Tundish operator to start preheating sequence using gas burners. Target temperature 1100-1200°C measured at tundish bottom. Minimum preheating time 4 hours - do not rush this step as inadequate preheating causes refractory thermal shock and failure. SAFETY CRITICAL: Verify all gas connections are tight and burner flames are stable before leaving unattended.

Step 3: Water system startup by Utilities Operator
Utilities operator to start secondary cooling water pumps and verify flow to all zones. Check pressure gauges read normal range (4.5-5.5 bar). Inspect all spray nozzles for blockages. Water quality must meet specifications (conductivity <10 μS/cm).

Step 4: Mould cooling water activation by CCM Operator
CCM operator to start primary mould cooling water circulation. Flow rate must achieve 800-1000 litres per minute. Verify inlet and outlet temperatures are within 5°C difference (indicating good flow distribution). Check for any leaks in mould cooling circuits.

Step 5: Hydraulic system check by CCM Operator
Verify hydraulic oil pressure for mould oscillation system (150-170 bar normal). Start oscillation mechanism and check stroke length and frequency match setpoints. Any abnormal noise or vibration must be investigated before proceeding.

Step 6: Dummy bar insertion by Maintenance Crew
Maintenance crew (minimum 2 persons) to manually insert dummy bar through entire casting length. Verify dummy bar head is properly positioned in mould and secured. Withdrawal pinch rolls to be engaged gently on dummy bar.

Step 7: Control system initialisation by Panel Operator
Panel operator to initialise CCM automation system, verify all interlocks are healthy, and no alarm conditions exist. Set casting speed to minimum (0.8 metres/min) for initial startup. Confirm communication with ladle tracking system and SMS control room.

Step 8: Final safety walkdown by Shift In-charge
Shift in-charge to conduct final walkdown of entire CCM area. Verify all personnel are clear of moving equipment zones, emergency stops are functional, and safety barriers are in place. Issue clearance for casting to commence.

Step 9: First ladle positioning and casting start by Ladle Crane Operator
Ladle crane operator to position first ladle over tundish. Tundish temperature must be above 1100°C before ladle opening. SMS coordinator to confirm ladle temperature is 1580-1620°C. Open ladle slowly and monitor initial metal flow into tundish.

SOP DOCUMENT 3: Slab Dimensional Inspection
---

SOP Number: QC-INSP-025
Title: Finished Slab Dimensional and Surface Quality Inspection
Issued by: Quality Assurance Department

Step 1: Slab identification and documentation by Quality Inspector
Inspector to verify slab identification number matches production records. Check heat number, grade, and customer order details on inspection sheet. SAFETY: Wear safety helmet, safety shoes, and hand gloves during inspection.

Step 2: Visual surface inspection by Quality Inspector  
Visually inspect all four surfaces (top, bottom, two edges) for surface defects including cracks, seams, slivers, scale formation, or depressions. Use inspection hammer to tap suspect areas and check for laminations. Mark any defect locations with yellow marking chalk.

Step 3: Dimensional measurements by Quality Inspector
Using calibrated steel tape measure, verify slab dimensions:
- Length: Measure at three points (both ends and centre), record all three readings
- Width: Measure at both ends and centre, record readings  
- Thickness: Measure at all four corners and centre (5 points total), record all readings
All measurements must be within tolerance specified on inspection sheet (typically ±3mm for length/width, ±1mm for thickness).

Step 4: Defect depth measurement by Quality Inspector
For any surface defects identified in Step 2, use depth gauge to measure defect depth. REJECTION CRITERIA: Defects deeper than 2mm on top/bottom surface or 3mm on edge surface require slab to be rejected or downgraded. Record defect type, location, and depth on inspection sheet.

Step 5: Surface temperature check by Quality Inspector
Use infrared thermometer to verify slab surface temperature is below 100°C before further handling. Hot slabs cannot be accurately measured and pose safety risk.

Step 6: Squareness and straightness check by Quality Inspector
Using straight edge and square, verify slab corners are square (90°±0.5°) and edges are straight (deviation <2mm per metre). Non-conforming slabs to be marked for cutting or conditioning.

Step 7: Documentation and disposition by Quality Inspector
Complete inspection report with all measurements and observations. Assign disposition:
- ACCEPT: Stamp slab with green paint, release for dispatch
- REWORK: Stamp with yellow paint, send to conditioning bay for grinding/scarfing
- REJECT: Stamp with red paint, quarantine for scrap or downgrade
Inspector to sign and date inspection sheet.

Step 8: Laboratory sample coordination by Quality Inspector
For slabs requiring chemical or mechanical property verification (as per sampling plan), mark slab for laboratory sampling and notify laboratory supervisor. Laboratory sample to be taken from designated location (typically slab end).

Step 9: Inspection record filing by Quality Inspector
Submit completed inspection sheet to quality control office within 2 hours. File inspection records by date and heat number. Update daily inspection summary in quality register.

SOP DOCUMENT 4: Confined Space Entry - Blast Furnace Bustle Main Inspection
---

SOP Number: SAFETY-CSE-008
Title: Confined Space Entry Procedure for BF Bustle Main and Hot Blast Stove Inspection
Category: SAFETY CRITICAL PROCEDURE
Authority: Plant Safety Officer

All confined space entry activities are high-risk and require strict compliance with this procedure. Violations may result in disciplinary action.

Step 1: Work permit application by Maintenance Supervisor
Maintenance supervisor to apply for Confined Space Entry Permit minimum 24 hours before planned entry. Permit application must specify location, nature of work, estimated duration, and number of personnel entering.

Step 2: Atmospheric testing by Safety Officer
SAFETY CRITICAL: Safety officer to conduct atmospheric testing using calibrated 4-gas monitor before entry permit is issued. Test for oxygen level (must be 19.5-23.5%), flammable gases (must be <10% LEL), carbon monoxide (<35 ppm), and hydrogen sulphide (<10 ppm). Testing must be done at multiple levels (top, middle, bottom) as gases stratify.

Step 3: Ventilation setup by Safety Team
If atmospheric testing shows deficiency, safety team to arrange forced ventilation using blowers. Minimum 30 minutes continuous ventilation required before re-testing. Ventilation to be maintained throughout entry period.

Step 4: Isolation and lockout by Electrical Team
SAFETY CRITICAL: Electrical team under Mr. Patel to isolate and lockout all energy sources (electrical, pneumatic, hydraulic) that could affect the confined space. Physical disconnection of hot blast valve is mandatory. Lockout tags to be applied by authorised personnel only.

Step 5: Communication system setup by Maintenance Crew
Establish communication system between personnel inside confined space and standby person outside. Two-way radio or sound-powered telephone required. Mobile phones are not acceptable as sole communication method.

Step 6: Rescue equipment staging by Safety Team
Safety team to stage rescue equipment at entry point including:
- Full body harness and retrieval line for each entrant
- Rescue tripod with winch (if vertical entry)
- Self-contained breathing apparatus (SCBA) - minimum 2 sets
- First aid kit and stretcher
SAFETY CRITICAL: Rescue equipment must be inspected and certified current before use.

Step 7: Permit issuance and briefing by Safety Officer
Safety officer to conduct toolbox talk with all personnel involved, explaining hazards, emergency procedures, and communication protocols. Issue Confined Space Entry Permit only after all preconditions met. Permit valid for single shift only - must be renewed for extended work.

Step 8: Entry and continuous monitoring by Entrant and Safety Watchman
Designated entrant wearing full body harness and connected to retrieval line to enter space. SAFETY CRITICAL: Safety watchman (trained in rescue) to maintain continuous visual or voice contact with entrant. Safety watchman must never enter space to attempt rescue - raise alarm and wait for rescue team.

Step 9: Periodic atmospheric re-testing by Safety Officer
Safety officer to re-test atmosphere every 2 hours during occupancy. Any adverse reading requires immediate evacuation and investigation before re-entry.

Step 10: Emergency evacuation procedure by All Personnel
On emergency alarm or communication loss, entrant to evacuate immediately using retrieval line. Safety watchman to raise alarm and not attempt entry. Emergency response team to don SCBA before any rescue attempt.

Step 11: Work completion and permit closure by Maintenance Supervisor
Upon work completion, maintenance supervisor to verify all personnel and tools have exited confined space. Remove all temporary equipment, restore isolation points, and return permit to safety office with completion sign-off.

SOP DOCUMENT 5: Quality Hold and Rejection Process
---

SOP Number: QC-PROC-015
Title: Non-Conforming Product Quality Hold and Disposition
Effective: January 2026

Step 1: Non-conformance identification by Any Personnel
Any personnel (operator, inspector, supervisor) identifying non-conforming product (outside specification limits) must immediately mark the material with RED FLAG tape and notify shift in-charge. Do not allow material to proceed to next operation.

Step 2: Immediate containment by Shift In-charge
Shift in-charge to physically segregate non-conforming material to quarantine area. Update production tracking system to reflect quality hold status. Notify quality control supervisor within 30 minutes of identification.

Step 3: Non-conformance documentation by Quality Inspector
Quality inspector to create Non-Conformance Report (NCR) documenting:
- Material identification (heat number, product code)
- Nature of non-conformance with measurements
- Quantity affected
- Suspected root cause
NCR to be raised in quality management system within 2 hours of identification.

Step 4: Material review board convening by Quality Control Manager
For significant non-conformances (>10 tonnes or customer-specific material), quality control manager to convene Material Review Board (MRB) comprising quality manager, production manager, and technical services manager. MRB to meet within 24 hours of NCR.

Step 5: Root cause investigation by Production Supervisor
Production supervisor to investigate root cause using process data, operator inputs, and equipment status. Document findings in NCR. Identify immediate corrective actions to prevent recurrence.

Step 6: Disposition decision by MRB or Quality Manager
Material Review Board or quality manager (for minor non-conformances) to assign disposition:
- REWORK: Material can be reprocessed to meet specifications
- USE AS IS: Minor deviation, customer concession obtained, material acceptable  
- SCRAP: Material cannot meet specifications, to be scrapped
- RETURN TO SUPPLIER: If raw material issue, coordinate with procurement

Step 7: Customer notification by Sales Coordinator
CRITICAL: If non-conformance affects customer order or delivery, sales coordinator must notify customer within 4 hours of disposition decision. Document customer response and any agreed actions.

Step 8: Corrective action implementation by Production Manager
Production manager to implement corrective actions identified in Step 5. Actions may include process parameter changes, equipment maintenance, operator retraining, or raw material specification changes. Target completion within 7 days of NCR.

Step 9: Material disposition execution by Relevant Department
Based on MRB decision:
- REWORK: Production planning to schedule reprocessing
- SCRAP: Dispatch to scrap yard with proper documentation  
- RETURN: Procurement to coordinate with supplier
Quality inspector to verify disposition is executed correctly and update NCR status.

Step 10: Effectiveness verification by Quality Manager
Quality manager to review NCR closure and verify corrective actions have been effective (no repeat non-conformances for 30 days). Close NCR in system and file for future reference and trend analysis.

SOP DOCUMENT 6: Overhead Crane Safety Checklist
---

SOP Number: SAFETY-CRANE-002
Title: Overhead Crane Pre-operation Safety Inspection
Applies to: All overhead cranes >10 tonne capacity

SAFETY CRITICAL PROCEDURE - Must be completed before every shift

Step 1: Visual inspection by Crane Operator
SAFETY CRITICAL: Crane operator to conduct visual walkround inspection before taking control. Check for:
- Wire rope condition (no broken wires, kinks, or excessive wear)
- Hook condition (no cracks, deformation, or missing safety latch)
- Load chain condition (if chain hoist type)
- Any oil leaks from gearbox or hydraulic system
- Condition of runway rails and end stops
Any defect identified must be reported immediately to maintenance supervisor. Do not operate crane if defects found.

Step 2: Control system check by Crane Operator
Test all controls before commencing operations:
- Emergency stop buttons (test both pendant and cabin e-stop)
- Hoist up/down controls with no load
- Bridge travel controls (forward/reverse)
- Trolley travel controls (left/right)
- Limit switches (upper/lower hoist limits must function correctly)
All controls must respond smoothly without sticking or delays.

Step 3: Warning systems test by Crane Operator
Activate crane warning bell/siren and verify audible throughout crane operational area. Warning must be given before any crane movement when personnel are in area.

Step 4: Load test with light load by Crane Operator
Perform test lift with light load (approximately 1-2 tonnes) before handling production loads. Observe for unusual noises, vibrations, or jerky movements. Test all directions of travel with light load.

Step 5: Documentation completion by Crane Operator
Complete Pre-operation Checklist form with date, time, crane number, and operator signature. Note any defects or observations. Submit checklist to shift supervisor.

Step 6: Defect reporting and tagging by Maintenance Supervisor
If any defects identified that prevent crane operation, maintenance supervisor to attach RED DANGER TAG to crane controls stating "CRANE OUT OF SERVICE - DO NOT OPERATE" with details of defect and supervisor signature.

Step 7: Safe working load verification by Crane Operator
Before lifting any load, operator to verify load weight is within crane Safe Working Load (SWL) rating. When in doubt, consult load charts posted in crane cabin. NEVER exceed SWL - this is safety critical.

Step 8: Rigging inspection by Rigger/Slinger
SAFETY CRITICAL: Designated rigger to inspect all rigging equipment (slings, shackles, hooks) before use. Check for:
- Sling identification tags are legible and current (colour code system)
- No cuts, abrasions, or burns on synthetic slings
- No kinks, bird caging, or broken wires on wire rope slings
- Shackles and hooks have no deformation or cracks
- Load capacity markings are clearly visible
Defective rigging to be removed from service immediately and tagged for disposal.
